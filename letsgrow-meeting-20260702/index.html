LetsGrow.com Meeting Script

MANNA CEA / MESH introduction and cooperation discussion · July 2, 2026

1. Meeting Goal

오늘 미팅의 목표는 바로 판매가 아니라, LetsGrow.com과 MESH가 어디에서 겹치고 어디에서 보완되는지 확인하는 것입니다. 핵심 포지션은 “MESH is a physical device, sensor, and modular automation layer that can make farm data AI-ready.”
  • First invite LetsGrow.com to explain their platform, market focus, and current collaboration model.
  • Then introduce MANNA CEA and MESH only after understanding their perspective.
  • Explain that MESH is not trying to replace LetsGrow.com.
  • Explore possible data / device / pilot-site collaboration.
  • Invite LetsGrow.com to consider a selected partner pilot discussion if there is a practical overlap.

2. Opening

Hi Martin, thank you for making time today. It was a pleasure meeting you at GreenTech Amsterdam.

Before I introduce MANNA CEA and MESH in detail, I would actually like to learn more about LetsGrow.com first.

I did some research online and I understand that LetsGrow.com has a strong position in data-driven growing, greenhouse data platforms, crop strategy, and decision support. But I still have many questions, especially about how you work with growers in practice and where you see the biggest needs in the market.

So if it is okay with you, I would be glad to first hear more about LetsGrow.com, your current focus, and how you see possible collaboration areas. After that, I can explain where MESH may fit and where we might be able to complement each other.

3. Short MANNA CEA Introduction

MANNA CEA is a Korean controlled-environment agriculture company founded in 2013.

We have exported turnkey growing solutions to around ten countries, including greenhouse and hydroponic systems. Through these projects, we learned that many growers need more than hardware. They need a practical way to connect sensors, control devices, farm operation records, and decision support.

That is why we are now focusing on MESH, our AI-based farm automation and management product.

4. MESH 60-Second Explanation

MESH is a modular platform for greenhouse monitoring, control integration, and AI-ready farm operation.

It connects environmental sensors, control modules, third-party devices, farm data, and App/Web dashboards. The idea is to help growers start from simple monitoring, then gradually expand toward semi-automatic control, operation records, alerts, and AI-based recommendations.

We recently received the GreenTech Amsterdam 2026 Innovation Awards Concept Award for MESH. For us, the award was meaningful because it confirmed that small and mid-size growers also need a more accessible automation and data layer.

5. Positioning Toward LetsGrow.com

LetsGrow.com 앞에서는 경쟁보다 보완 관계를 강조하는 것이 안전합니다. LetsGrow는 데이터/재배전략/의사결정 지원에 강하고, MESH는 현장의 센서·장비·제어 모듈·로컬 운영 연결에 강하다고 말하면 됩니다.
From what I understand, LetsGrow.com has strong expertise in data-driven growing, crop strategy, centralizing greenhouse data, and decision support for growers.

MESH is slightly different in its starting point. We are closer to the physical farm layer: sensors, local devices, control modules, integration modules, and field operation workflows.

So rather than seeing this as a replacement, I think there may be an interesting complementary area. LetsGrow.com is strong in using data for growing decisions, while MESH can help make physical farm devices and field data easier to connect and operate.

6. Questions To Ask LetsGrow.com

  • How does LetsGrow.com usually collect data from growers' existing greenhouse systems?
  • Do you normally rely on climate computers, sensors, ERP systems, or manual input?
  • Where do you still see gaps in field-level data collection?
  • Are small and mid-size growers also a target market for LetsGrow.com?
  • Would a modular device layer like MESH be useful in markets where existing climate computers are limited or fragmented?
  • Do you see potential for technical exchange, pilot sites, or data integration discussion?

7. Selected Partner Pilot Support Program

첨부 이미지의 프로그램 설명용 멘트입니다. 무료 배포 행사처럼 들리면 안 됩니다. “적합한 파트너와 1개 사이트에서 검증하는 제한적 지원 프로그램”이라고 말하는 게 좋습니다.
We are also preparing a selected partner pilot support program for MESH in 2026.

This is not a free product distribution campaign. It is a limited pilot support program for selected partners who have a suitable site and a clear use case where MESH can be tested properly.

The typical support scope may include a small pilot kit, such as an AI Hub, a small control box, limited FR/SW control channels, Air Nano sensors, and one MESH Link, depending on the actual site conditions.

The goal is to verify practical applicability at one real site, collect feedback, and then discuss whether there is a longer-term cooperation model.

Soft Invitation

If LetsGrow.com sees a suitable partner grower, research site, or integration scenario, we would be open to discussing whether this pilot support program could be relevant.

It could be a good way to test how MESH field data, device integration, and LetsGrow.com's data-driven growing perspective might work together in practice.

8. If They Ask: “Are You Competing With LetsGrow.com?”

That is a fair question. I think there may be some overlap in the broad area of farm data and decision support.

But our current focus is more on the physical and operational layer: connecting sensors, control modules, third-party equipment, and farm operation records into one AI-ready system.

We are not here to replace LetsGrow.com's expertise in data-driven growing. I am more interested in understanding whether there are complementary areas, especially in markets or farms where device-level integration is still difficult.

9. If They Ask: “What Data Can MESH Provide?”

MESH can collect environmental data such as temperature, humidity, VPD-related values, and sensor data from connected devices. Depending on the configuration, it can also connect root-zone sensors, weather data, irrigation or equipment events, and selected control module status.

The exact data scope depends on the installed sensors, controller interfaces, and validated communication protocols. We usually separate what is available immediately, what can be integrated after validation, and what requires additional development.

10. If They Ask: “Can You Integrate With Existing Systems?”

In principle, yes, but we are careful about this point.

For third-party equipment, we need to confirm the exact model, communication port, protocol, register map, access rights, and safety conditions. MESH Link can support integration, but we do not assume compatibility before technical validation.

For a first pilot, we often recommend starting with independent monitoring first, then adding read-only integration or control functions step by step.

11. If They Ask: “What Are You Looking For From LetsGrow.com?”

At this stage, I am mainly looking for technical exchange and market perspective.

I would like to understand where LetsGrow.com sees the biggest gaps in greenhouse data collection and grower-facing decision support. If there is a clear complementary area, we could later discuss a pilot, data integration, or a joint use case with a suitable grower.

12. If English Gets Difficult

Let me explain this a little more simply.

MESH is not only software. It connects the physical farm: sensors, control devices, and operation data. Then this data can be used for monitoring, alerts, records, and AI-based recommendations.

Our question is whether this physical farm layer could be useful together with LetsGrow.com's data-driven growing expertise.
